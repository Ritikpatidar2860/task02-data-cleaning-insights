1. Introduction

In this project I worked on a retail sales dataset that had many problems like missing values, duplicate rows, spelling mistakes in category names, and dates written in different formats. My goal was to first clean all these issues and then find useful business insights from the clean data.

The raw dataset had 1,250 rows. After cleaning I was left with 1,199 correct rows that were ready for analysis.

3. Cleaning Steps

Step 1 — Removing Duplicates

I removed 35 exact duplicate rows and 15 rows where the same Order ID was entered again with slightly different spelling. In total 50 duplicate entries were removed.

Step 2 — Fixing Missing Values

  • Revenue was missing in some rows — I calculated it using Price x Quantity x Discount
  • Quantity was missing — I filled it with the average quantity for that product category
  • Discount Percent was missing — I filled it with 0 meaning no discount was given
  • City, Region, Payment Method missing — filled with the word Unknown
  • Customer Rating left blank where customer did not rate — added a HasRating column
  • 1 row was deleted because both Price and Revenue were missing

Step 3 — Fixing Text and Formatting

Category names like electronics, ELECTRONICS and Electronic were all mapped to one correct spelling Electronics. Dates were written in 5 different formats across the dataset — I converted all of them to YYYY-MM-DD format. Names and cities had extra spaces which were removed and converted to proper title case.

Step 4 — Outlier Detection

I used the IQR method to find unusual values. For prices and revenue I checked outliers category by category because a high price in Electronics is normal but the same price in Books would be a mistake.

6. What I Think the Business Should Do

  • Electronics is the most important category — always keep good stock
  • Books have most orders but low revenue — try bundling or upsell
  • Offer small cashback on UPI to move customers away from Cash on Delivery
  • March and October had high sales — prepare extra inventory in these months
  • Focus marketing in Kochi, Lucknow and Bhubaneswar — they are performing very well
  • Check the 7 price outlier orders with the data entry team

8. What I Learned

This project taught me that real world data is never clean. Even a simple sales file can have missing values, spelling mistakes, wrong formats and duplicate entries all at the same time.

The most important thing I learned is that you should not just delete rows when something is missing. Instead try to recover the value from other columns when possible. I also learned that outliers should be flagged and not always deleted.

Overall this was a good learning experience and I now feel more confident about handling messy data in future projects.
